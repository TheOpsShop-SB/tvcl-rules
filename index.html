Before the match

The toss must take place no earlier than 30 minutes and no later than 15 minutes before the 12:30pm start. Both Captains and any Umpires are expected to attend.

If the designated Captain hasn't arrived but at least 7 players from each team are present, the toss must proceed without them. Play then starts within 15 minutes.

At the toss, Captains must agree:

  • Drink break timing — normally at the midpoint of each innings
  • Wide ball interpretation — if no 17-inch crease marking is on the field

Balls are inspected and team sheets exchanged:

  • Home Team provides 2 new TVCL balls + 2 spare new + at least 6 other spare TVCL balls — red ball for both divisions
  • Once the match starts, no player changes are permitted
If the Home Team cannot provide 2 new TVCL balls, the match may continue with lower grade balls only if Umpires and both Captains agree — but the Home Team forfeits the toss.

Team sheet Penalty

Complete the team sheet before the toss. No player may appear as 'Unregistered', 'Unsure' or 'Free Text' on the Play-Cricket scorecard. Each team with ineligible players receives a 5 penalty point deduction.

Youth players

All players aged Under 19 or younger must be declared to the Captain, Umpires and Scorers before the match — whether they are bowling or not.

30-yard ring Home

Must be marked in both divisions: two semi-circles of 30 yards radius from each middle stump, joined by straight lines, marked with dots at approximately 5-yard intervals.

Clothing

Both divisions: predominantly white. Kit, armbands and visible undergarments must not be the same or similar colour as the red match ball.

Panel Umpires are only appointed for Divisions 1, 1B and 2. Each team in Div 6 and Div 8 is expected to provide a League Registered Umpire or Club Umpire. If neither team has one, the Captains jointly take responsibility.

Bonus point: each team providing a League Registered or Panel Umpire earns 1 bonus point — provided they officiate the full match, are present at the toss, and are named on the Play-Cricket scorecard by 10:00pm Wednesday. Unregistered Club Umpires and Player Umpires do not qualify.
Falsely claiming a League Registered Umpire: first offence = 5 penalty points; second and subsequent = 9 penalty points each time.

The toss cannot take place until the pitch has been prepared and marked by the Grounds Authority.

If the pitch is altered for any reason after the toss but before the match starts, the Umpires arrange a new toss and delay the start accordingly. Once the match has commenced, no retoss takes place.

No ball triggers

  • Bowler oversteps the crease
  • Short-pitched delivery that bounces and clearly passes over head height of the batter standing upright at the crease
  • Fielding restriction breach (leg side or ring)

The Home Team must make every effort to get the ground ready for the original start and continue doing so up to 4:00pm. If no play has been possible by 4:00pm, the match is declared a cancelled washout.

A team with 5 or fewer players at the venue at the latest start time is deemed to have forfeited the match.

Matches cannot be called off due to weather before 4:00pm, except in specific circumstances:

Between 8am and 11:30am

The Home Team may advise the Away Team not to travel — but must offer them the option to come and inspect. If the Away Team agrees no play is possible and declines to travel, the Home Team submits the result as a cancelled washout.

After 11:30am — no Panel or League Registered Umpire present

Both Captains must agree. If either believes play can start by 4:00pm, the match cannot be cancelled until that time. If the toss hasn't happened, the decision is taken 15 minutes before 4:00pm.

Weather forecasts must not be used when assessing fitness for play. The decision must be based on actual ground conditions at the time.

Interruption in the 1st innings

The first 30 cumulative minutes lost are ignored. After that, 2 overs are deducted (1 per innings) for every completed 7 minutes lost. At the end of the 1st innings, a revised target is calculated using the DLS method.

Interruption in the 2nd innings

Once the 30-minute allowance is used up, 1 over is deducted for every completed 3.5 minutes lost — down to a minimum of 20 overs. Every reduction triggers a new DLS target. Umpires confirm the revised target with Scorers and inform both Captains before resuming.

1st innings cut-off — no rain interruption

If there has been no rain interruption, the 1st innings is deemed completed at 4:00pm — provided at least 20 overs have been bowled. Any over in progress at 4:00pm is completed. The 2nd innings receives the same number of completed overs.

DLS calculations are done using Play-Cricket Scorer. The team batting second is responsible for updating the scoreboard including the par score.

The TVCL follows the ECB 30/30 rule:

  • Suspend play and seek shelter immediately when the gap between lightning flash and thunder is 30 seconds or less
  • Safe to return 30 minutes after the last flash or clap of thunder — only if Umpires agree the storm has passed
  • Earlier return possible if both Umpires agree the storm has clearly moved on
Do not shelter under trees. Do not go near metal-framed pitch covers or machinery. Move indoors and spread out — do not bunch together.

A tie is declared when the scores are level or 1 run less than the target at the completion of the match — regardless of wickets lost. Both teams receive 14 points. No bonus points are available on a tie.

Priority rule

If the club cannot field all teams on the same day, the highest division fixture always takes priority. If this rule is breached, all lower teams also forfeit their matches.

For scorers

The mandatory scorer rule — with a 1 point deduction — applies only in Divisions 1, 1B and 2. There is no penalty for Div 6 or Div 8 for not providing a dedicated scorer.

That said, a scorer is strongly recommended and the TVCL endorses Play-Cricket Scorer Pro. A scorer who cannot keep an accurate scorebook, acknowledge Umpire signals correctly, or assist with DLS timing is considered incompetent for the purposes of the Umpire's match report.

The scorer should be briefed by the Umpires before play: confirm match format, hours of play, boundary allowances, master clock, display of runs/wickets/overs, and pre-signals.
  • Keep an accurate scorebook — online (Play-Cricket Scorer Pro) or hard copy
  • Acknowledge signals from the Umpires during play
  • Keep a check on the score jointly with the Umpires
  • Display runs, wickets and overs visibly from the pitch
  • Note the time of all stoppages — essential for over-rate calculations and DLS

DLS par score display — Division 6 Div 6

The scorer (or DLS Manager) must display the par score visibly from the pitch at the end of each over and at the fall of each wicket in the 2nd innings — even if there has been no loss of overs. Failure to do so is reported to the Management Committee and repeated failures result in a 5 penalty point deduction.

DLS par score display — Division 8 Div 8

The team batting second is responsible for regularly updating the scoreboard including the par score using Play-Cricket Scorer.

Spare balls are ideally kept with the Scorers under their control, with restricted access. Umpires will confirm this at the toss.

Both Home and Away Captains submit a CMRF on TVCL24 by 11:00pm Monday covering:

  • Match status — select the correct result from the dropdown
  • Pitch and outfield marks — Away Captain only. Default 3 = Acceptable. Score 2 or below requires an explanation. Key criterion for pitch: consistency of bounce. Key criterion for outfield: whether fielders could attack the ball safely.
  • Fair play mark — rate the opposition 1–10. Marks below 4 require an explanation. This does not trigger a disciplinary process.
Fair play marks and formal disciplinary reports are separate. A low fair play mark alone does not start disciplinary proceedings — report conduct incidents separately via the TVCL disciplinary process.

In Divisions 6 and 8, in-match conduct penalties are not applied by Umpires. Misconduct is reported after the match through the TVCL disciplinary process.

Captains are responsible for the behaviour of their players at all times. A report goes to the league and the opposition team; the opposition is expected to deal with it promptly.

Note details during the match — names, over, ball, nature of the incident. The report is submitted after the match, not during it.
